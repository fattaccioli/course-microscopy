10  Resources

A curated set of references, tools, and datasets to complement the course. Resources are also listed at the end of each chapter with more specific pointers.

10.1 Textbooks

  • Lakowicz, J. Principles of Fluorescence Spectroscopy, 3rd ed. Springer (2006). — The reference on fluorophore photophysics, FRET, FLIM, and quenching.
  • Murphy & Davidson. Fundamentals of Light Microscopy and Electronic Imaging, 2nd ed. Wiley (2012). — Clear, practical introduction to the full microscopy chain.
  • Pawley, J. (ed.) Handbook of Biological Confocal Microscopy, 3rd ed. Springer (2006). — The comprehensive technical reference; every chapter by a specialist.
  • Valeur & Berberan-Santos. Molecular Fluorescence: Principles and Applications, 2nd ed. Wiley-VCH (2012). — Deeper photophysics than Lakowicz; excellent on time-resolved methods.
  • Masters & So (eds.) Handbook of Biomedical Nonlinear Optical Microscopy. Oxford University Press (2008). — Two-photon, SHG, THG, CARS.

10.3 Online Resources & Tools

10.3.1 Spectra & Filter Design

  • FPbase Spectra Viewer — absorption/emission spectra for any fluorophore; overlay laser lines and filters.
  • Semrock Searchlight — the most complete filter database; design filter sets with your hardware.
  • Thermo Fisher SpectraViewer — fluorophore/filter/laser overlay.
  • Chroma Spectra Viewer — alternative filter design tool.

10.3.2 Fluorescent Protein Database

  • FPbase — curated database of all fluorescent proteins: spectra, quantum yields, bleaching rates, FRET pair calculator, microscope simulator.

10.3.3 Learning

  • MicroscopyU (Nikon) — illustrated tutorials on every aspect of optical microscopy.
  • Molecular Expressions (FSU) — optics and microscopy reference with interactive applets.
  • iBiology Microscopy Series — video lectures by leading microscopists (Betzig, Zhuang, Webb, Bhattacharya, and others).
  • EMBL EIPP Courses — advanced image processing and analysis courses; materials available online.

10.3.4 Image Analysis Software

  • Fiji / ImageJ — the standard open-source platform; essential plugins: Bio-Formats, MTrackJ, Coloc2, ThunderSTORM (dSTORM reconstruction).
  • CellProfiler — automated pipeline-based image analysis for high-content data.
  • µManager — open-source microscope control software.
  • napari — Python-based n-dimensional image viewer; increasingly the standard for large datasets.

10.3.5 Super-Resolution Analysis

  • SMLM challenge — benchmark datasets and software comparison for single-molecule localization.
  • ThunderSTORM — Fiji plugin for PALM/STORM/dSTORM reconstruction.

10.4 Datasets

  • Cell Image Library — curated fluorescence microscopy images and movies with biological annotations.
  • BBBC — Broad Bioimage Benchmark Collection — annotated image sets for algorithm benchmarking.
  • SMLM benchmark datasets — simulated and experimental single-molecule localization datasets.
  • Allen Cell Image Collection — 3D fluorescence images of hiPSC cells with labeled organelles.

10.5 Practical Guides

  • Waters, J.C. Accuracy and precision in quantitative fluorescence microscopy. J. Cell Biol. (2009). DOI
  • North, A.J. Seeing is believing? A beginners’ guide to practical pitfalls in image acquisition. J. Cell Biol. (2006). DOI
  • Haugland, R. The Molecular Probes Handbook, 11th ed. Available online
